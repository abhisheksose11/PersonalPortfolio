Postdoctoral Researcher

Abhishek T. Sose

University of Maryland, College Park

Computational–experimental materials scientist specializing in AI-enabled, autonomous materials discovery — spanning molecular simulations to robot-assisted synthesis. Expertise in MD, DFT/AIMD, machine learning, and machine-learned interatomic potentials (MLIPs), with a strong focus on Bayesian uncertainty quantification and closed-loop optimization. Currently developing AI-guided, self-driving laboratory workflows for the predictive design and synthesis of composite aerogels with multi-property tuning.

Vision

Materials Discovery

🤖

Automation

AI Agentic Approach for Simulations

Developing autonomous AI agents that orchestrate complex multi-step simulation pipelines—ranging from structure generation to property prediction—effectively reducing the "human-in-the-loop" bottleneck in high-throughput workflows.

AI-ML Driven Robotic Experiments

Integrating machine learning with lab automation to close the loop between virtual discovery and physical synthesis, specifically applied to the discovery of sustainable, all-natural plastic substitutes.

Generative AI for Science

Fine-tuning LLMs, building AI agents, and Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) for accelerated scientific discovery and automated literature synthesis.

⚛️

Forcefield Development

BUQ-driven MLIPs

Utilizing Bayesian Uncertainty Quantification to develop robust Machine Learning Interatomic Potentials (MLIPs) that "know what they don't know," ensuring reliable energy predictions in unexplored chemical spaces.

AA- & CG- Forcefields with BUQ

Developing All-Atom (AA) and Coarse-Grained (CG) forcefields that incorporate BUQ insights to bridge the gap between microscopic accuracy and macroscopic simulation timescales.

📈

Uncertainty Propagation

From Atoms to Experiments

Establishing rigorous frameworks for propagating uncertainty from the DFT level through MD simulations up to experimental validation, ensuring the reliability of materials discovery pipelines.

End-to-End Research Architecture

A "Full-Stack" approach to materials science: seamlessly transitioning from quantum mechanics (DFT) to molecular dynamics (MD), finite element analysis (FEA), and finally to robotic experimental verification.

ML-Enhanced Transition State Theory

Applying TST within machine learning frameworks to predict reaction kinetics and long-term material stability, bridging the gap between molecular transitions and experimental degradation rates.
